Susan Jane Reppuhn

1947 - 2018

There are lots of facts that get talked about at the end of a person's life. How old, where did they work, where did they live, an inventory of family members and spouses past and present. None of which tells you anything about that person.

What matters most is countless unmeasurable things about a person's life that don't get mentioned.

Do you know in February my mom crocheted a scarf for each kid in Chase's class. All different colors in a paw print pattern so they would have each have something a little special for Valentines Day.

When Hudson was in the NICU she sat in the hospital with me everyday so I wasn't alone while Scott worked.

She had made and saved little balls of yarn in a bin where I found a pattern to crochet blankets for those in wheelchairs. A bin a baby overalls she was going to convert into bags people could hang on their walkers.

My mom's heart and compassion were unmeasurable. There will never be another person like her. I was blessed to have her as my mother. My days from this point on will never be the same, but I will carry her with each and every step I take.
